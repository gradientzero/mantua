**Distillation** trains a small "student" model to reproduce the behavior of a large
"teacher" model, typically on a task-specific distribution of inputs. It is the main
reason [[small-language-models]] can be credible on narrow enterprise tasks: the student
doesn't need the teacher's breadth, only its competence on one slice of work.

## Rough taxonomy (to be expanded)

- **Response-based**: student learns from teacher outputs (the common, API-compatible case).
- **Feature-based**: student matches internal representations — requires white-box access to the teacher.
- **Task-specific synthetic data**: teacher generates labeled training data rather than being imitated directly; often the pragmatic choice when the teacher is a third-party API with restrictive terms.
