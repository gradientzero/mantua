The pull toward private deployment is rarely about raw capability — frontier APIs win
that comparison. It is about three things:

1. **Data governance.** Regulated industries (health, finance, defense, legal) often
   cannot send working data to a third-party API at all, or only under agreements that
   take quarters to negotiate.
2. **Cost predictability.** Owned inference converts a variable per-token bill into
   amortized hardware — attractive exactly where [[small-language-models]] shine:
   high-volume, repetitive, industry-specific tasks.
3. **Roadmap independence.** Model deprecations and pricing changes upstream become
   someone else's problem when the weights are yours, which usually means an open-weights
   model adapted via [[model-distillation|distillation]] or fine-tuning.

## What this costs

Private deployment moves the operational burden in-house: serving infrastructure,
monitoring, evaluation, and model updates. For many organizations that trade is only
worth it past a volume threshold — locating that threshold per industry is one of the
core goals of this research project.
